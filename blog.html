Generative adversarial networks (GAN)

May 20, 2020

Generative adversarial networks (GANs) are architectures that use two neural networks Generator(G) and Discriminator(D), pitting one against the other (thus the “adversarial”) in order to generate new, synthetic instances of data that can pass for real data. Generating model Collect the distribution of snapshot sample data, so the input is random noise signal and output is generated image. The discriminant model is a binary classifier, its input is the real sample and the generated sample, and the output is the determination of the input category real or fake. The optimization directions of D and G are opposite, D hopes to be able to distinguish between real data and generated samples. G hopes to be able to generate fake samples with uncertain discrimination to deceive D. Accompanied by alternating evolutions, D and G eventually acquired good abilities at the same time.

In the perfect equilibrium, the generator would capture the general training data distribution. As a result, the discriminator is always unsure of whether its inputs are real or not.
